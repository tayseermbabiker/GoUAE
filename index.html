Prescription Medications & Drug Laws Guide

Reality Check: UAE customs are very reasonable with legitimate medications. 99% of tourists have zero issues when following basic rules.

Zero Tolerance Items
  • CBD oil/products - Including hemp, even if legal in your country
  • Medical marijuana - No exceptions, even with prescriptions
  • Poppy seeds - Can trigger drug tests, avoid entirely
  • Tramadol without prescription - Common painkiller, strictly controlled
Generally Allowed (With Proper Documentation)
  • Prescription medications - Original containers + pharmacy receipt
  • Over-the-counter drugs - Reasonable personal quantities (30-day supply)
  • Insulin & diabetes supplies - Bring prescription or medical ID
  • Antidepressants/anxiety meds - With prescription, no quantity limits
  • Blood pressure medications - Very common, rarely questioned
  • Inhalers & asthma medications - Essential medical devices allowed
Needs Extra Documentation
  • Controlled substances - Doctor letter + original prescription required
  • Large quantities - More than 30-day supply needs explanation
  • Liquid medications - Follow aviation rules + bring prescription
  • Narcotics/opioids - Valid prescription essential, keep quantities minimal

Fear: "What if I have drug traces in my system?"

Reality: UAE doesn't randomly drug test tourists. Only tested if arrested for other reasons. Normal prescription use is not an issue.

Fear: "Will customs search all my medications?"

Reality: Most tourists walk through green channel with no checks. Having prescriptions in original containers is sufficient for 99% of cases.

Fear: "What about my antidepressants/anxiety meds?"

Reality: Mental health medications are completely normal and accepted. UAE has modern healthcare system.

  • Local people - Always ask before photographing anyone
  • Inside mosques - Some allow photos, others don't - just ask
  • Museums & galleries - Policies vary, check with staff
  • Private properties - Hotels might have rules about public areas

Avoid Photographing
  • Government/military buildings - Airports security areas, police stations

  • Accidents or incidents - Emergency situations, traffic accidents
